💡 What Oddly actually is

Not a gig platform. A neighbourhood trust network.

Young people have been mowing lawns, walking dogs, and helping neighbours for generations. Oddly doesn't change that relationship — it makes it safer, more transparent, and easier to manage. The relationship stays between your child, you, and the neighbour. Oddly is the trust layer that connects them.

👨‍👩‍👧
You control the narrative
You decide who your child works for, how far they can go, how many hours they work, and what jobs they take. Oddly creates the network. You control it.
🔍
Every neighbour is verified
Before a neighbour can post a job, they verify their identity and address. You see their profile, community history, and any endorsements from other verified families before approving.
📈
It's good for them
Every job builds a real verified CV, teaches money management, and builds confidence. By 18 your child will have years of real work experience — plus savings and financial education built in.
🛡️ Your controls

Everything you need to feel confident.

These aren't optional add-ons. Every one of these controls is built into the core of Oddly — because safety isn't a feature, it's the foundation.

📍
Radius control
Set the maximum distance from home. Your child only ever sees jobs within that radius. 1 km, 5 km, or custom — your call, changeable anytime.
✅
Employer approval
Every first job with a new neighbour requires your explicit approval. You see their verified profile, address, community history, and endorsements before deciding. One tap to approve or decline.
🗺️
Live location during jobs
See exactly where your child is while any job is active. You're notified the moment they arrive and the moment they leave. A quiet check-in ping goes out every 30 minutes on longer jobs.

Every active job has a discreet support button. If your child taps it — "I'm uncomfortable", "Something isn't right", "Please contact me" — you get an immediate notification and their location. The job can be paused instantly.

❓ FAQ

Questions parents ask us.

What age can my child join?
The Oddly Crew is open to young people aged 13–17. Every account requires a linked parent or guardian account — your child cannot join without you.
What if something goes wrong during a job?
Your child has a "Need Help" button on every active job. Tapping it immediately notifies you with their location and lets them pause the job. You can contact them or the neighbour directly through the app.
How do I know the neighbour is safe?
Every neighbour verifies their identity and address before posting a job. You see their full profile — including community years, completed jobs hosted, and endorsements from other verified families — before approving. You can decline any employer, any time, for any reason.
What jobs are allowed?
Jobs are limited to outdoor and community-facing tasks — lawn mowing, dog walking, car washing, garden tidying, grocery carrying, snow clearing, and similar. No indoor unsupervised jobs at launch.
How does payment work?
The neighbour pays upfront before the job starts. Funds are held securely by Oddly and released to your child only when the job is confirmed complete. You see the agreed price before approving. Your child always receives 100% of the agreed amount.
What happens to negative feedback?
All feedback is reviewed by Oddly before your child sees it. Harsh or unfair reviews are rewritten into constructive, developmental language. There are no star ratings. Your child will never see a crushing review — only encouragement and specific things to improve.
Is Oddly free?
Yes — free to join for the Oddly Crew and parents. Neighbours pay a small 5% platform fee on top of the agreed job price. Your child always receives the full agreed amount.

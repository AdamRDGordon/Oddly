🏫 School partnerships

Learning that connects to real life.

Oddly Academy brings real-world work and financial education into schools. Not theory. Practical skills the Crew uses immediately.

💬
In-school workshops
Sessions on money management, negotiation basics, how to pitch for a job, and how to handle feedback. Run by Oddly educators in partnership with teachers.
📊
Curriculum tie-ins
Oddly supports existing work-readiness and school work-experience programmes with practical, trackable activity.
👩‍🏫
Teacher portal
Teachers can track student participation, earnings, and skill badges as a real-world project. Oddly becomes part of the classroom — not just something done after school.
🎤 Events

Meet people who built something from nothing.

Young Entrepreneur Days, pop-up job fairs, and guest speaker evenings — in your community, with real founders who started where you are.

🚀
Young Entrepreneur Days
Half-day community events with guest speakers, peer networking, and real-world entrepreneurship challenges. Earn XP for attending.
🏪
Pop-up job fairs
Neighbours come to meet the Crew in person and post jobs on the spot. The best way to build trust in a new neighbourhood. Earn XP for every job you land.
🎤
Guest speaker evenings
Real founders. Real stories. People who started businesses at 16, dropped out of uni, made mistakes, and built something anyway. Because hearing it is different from reading it.
📈 The entrepreneur path

Solo job to micro-business. Step by step.

The Oddly Academy path takes a first-time Crew member all the way to Oddly Alumni — mentoring the next generation of young entrepreneurs.

🌱
Solo — first jobs
Start simple. One job at a time. Build your reputation, earn your first badges, and get your first recurring client.
🤝
Team — bigger jobs
Senior Crew members can bring in friends for larger jobs. Split the earnings, share the XP, build something together.
🔁
Recurring — real clients
Turn one-off jobs into regular bookings. A recurring client is the closest thing to running a real small business — and it looks incredible on a CV.
🎓
Alumni — give back
At 18 you become Oddly Alumni. Mentor younger Crew members, speak at events, and help the next wave get started the way you did.
